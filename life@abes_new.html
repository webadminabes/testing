Library

The ABES library is a 1384 sq.m resource center with a 400 sq.m reading area, offering over 112,334 books, 1224 e-journals, 8074 e-books, and seating for 250. Fully automated since 2006 and using KOHA, it provides seamless access to resources, including remote e-resource login. Supporting self-learning and research, it is a key part of the academic environment.

Community Engagements

HUHC (Help Us to Help the Child)

Empowering through Education: Founded by ABES alumnus Shwet Prakash Srivastava in 2012, HUHC (Help Us to Help the Child) is a community dedicated to educating children in slum areas without access to schooling. Under the guidance of Dr. Devendra Kumar Arya, HUHC employs the Inclusive Tri-Educational Model (ITEM) to reach underserved areas.

Our initiatives include literacy centers where volunteers regularly teach children and Kiran Shiksha Sansthan, a School of Hope near ABES that offers a supportive, school-like environment. Join us in empowering disadvantaged children through education and helping them achieve their full potential.

LDL (Light De Literacy)

"Educate, Empower, Enlighten"

The Light De Literacy (LDL) initiative at ABES Engineering College is committed to identifying and educating underprivileged children, empowering them to become dignified and self-reliant members of society. Our motto, "Educate, Empower, Enlighten," reflects our mission to help children discover their hidden potential and become catalysts for social change.

LDL enables these children to grow, succeed, and shine as responsible citizens by providing quality education and support. With over 40 children admitted to schools, our program has seen remarkable achievements in academics and sports, fostering overall development and success.

Unnat Bharat Abhiyaan

Unnat Bharat Abhiyaan at ABESEC is inspired by the vision of transformational change in rural development processes by leveraging knowledge institutions to help build the architecture of an Inclusive India.

Under Unnat Bharat Abhiyaan Wing at ABESEC College was established on 2nd Oct. 2018 and it has constituted its organized structure. ABESEC has adopted 5 villages named as ‘Kushaliya, Kanauja, Matiala, Masuri and Mohiddinpur-Dabarasi’. Students of various technical domains along with their faculty mentors have visited these villages initially to complete the survey under predefined agenda. The survey was inclined as per structured format given by IIT-Delhi.
